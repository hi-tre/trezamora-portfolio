About

Maker by nature.
Builder by practice.
Thinker at heart.

I'm Tre, a Product Manager based in Honolulu, Hawaiʻi. I spend my days thinking about product problems, roadmaps, and why things do or don't get built. I spend my evenings doing something about as analog as it gets: beating bark into kapa, carving wood, or figuring out why a design isn't working at the material level.

That probably sounds like two different people. It's actually the same one.

The making mentality

I've been a maker my whole life. Hawaiian kapa practitioner, woodworker, visual artist. What that's given me professionally isn't just a creative eye. It's a specific kind of patience with process: the understanding that good work requires constraint, repetition, and a willingness to throw out what isn't working before it becomes load-bearing.

Kapa especially. The practice is pattern systems, material limitations, and cultural protocol all at once. You don't improvise your way through it. You understand the logic of the system first, then you work within it, and sometimes you find the edges worth pushing. That's exactly how I approach product.

The work

Ten years of embedded product work across Indigenous organizations, nonprofits, AgTech startups, and venture studios in Hawaiʻi. Currently an Applied Product Manager at Purple Maiʻa Foundation, which means I'm not just writing specs. I'm in the build environment alongside the people using what we ship.

My background spans UX/UI design and applied AI development. What that combination produces is a Product Manager who can operate across the full stack of the job: owning discovery, defining roadmaps, evaluating technical tradeoffs, and communicating decisions across product, design, and engineering without losing anything in translation. That's the Applied Product Lead capability, and it lives inside the Product Manager identity, not beside it.
